# Major Arcana Reference

## Overview

The 22 Major Arcana represent the archetypal journey of the soul - from The Fool's innocent potential through The World's integrated wholeness. In Jungian terms, this is the individuation process.

## The Cards

### 0 - The Fool

**Keywords**: Beginnings, innocence, spontaneity, free spirit
**Archetype**: The Divine Child
**Hermetic Principle**: Mentalism - reality begins in mind

**Upright**: New beginnings, leap of faith, innocence, spontaneity. The Fool stands at the precipice of adventure, unburdened by past failures. Trust the journey.

**Reversed**: Recklessness, naivety, foolishness, risk-taking. The shadow Fool rushes in without wisdom, or hesitates when the leap is required.

---

### I - The Magician

**Keywords**: Manifestation, power, action, resourcefulness
**Archetype**: The Self/Ego
**Hermetic Principle**: Correspondence - as above, so below

**Upright**: Manifestation, will, skill, resourcefulness. The Magician channels divine will into earthly form. All tools are available - will you use them?

**Reversed**: Manipulation, trickery, untapped potential, scattered energy. The shadow Magician is all talk, or uses power for deception.

---

### II - The High Priestess

**Keywords**: Intuition, mystery, unconscious, inner knowledge
**Archetype**: The Anima
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - the feminine principle

**Upright**: Intuition, mystery, the unconscious, sacred knowledge. She guards the threshold between known and unknown. What do you already know but haven't acknowledged?

**Reversed**: Secrets, disconnection from intuition, withdrawal. The shadow Priestess hoards mysteries or cannot access inner wisdom.

---

### III - The Empress

**Keywords**: Abundance, nurturing, nature, fertility
**Archetype**: The Great Mother
**Hermetic Principle**: Gender - creative principle

**Upright**: Abundance, nurturing, creation, sensuality. The Empress embodies fertile creativity and maternal care. What needs nurturing in your life?

**Reversed**: Dependence, creative block, neglect, smothering. The shadow Empress withholds nurture or overwhelms with it.

---

### IV - The Emperor

**Keywords**: Authority, structure, control, father figure
**Archetype**: The Father
**Hermetic Principle**: Gender - receptive principle

**Upright**: Authority, structure, control, stability. The Emperor builds lasting foundations through discipline. Where do you need more structure?

**Reversed**: Tyranny, rigidity, domination, lack of discipline. The shadow Emperor either crushes through control or crumbles without it.

---

### V - The Hierophant

**Keywords**: Tradition, conformity, spiritual wisdom, institutions
**Archetype**: The Wise Old Man
**Hermetic Principle**: Correspondence - spiritual/material bridge

**Upright**: Tradition, conformity, spiritual guidance, education. The Hierophant transmits sacred knowledge through established forms. What tradition serves you?

**Reversed**: Rebellion, subversion, new approaches, challenging convention. The shadow Hierophant clings to dead tradition or rejects all guidance.

---

### VI - The Lovers

**Keywords**: Union, choice, alignment, relationships
**Archetype**: The Syzygy (union of opposites)
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - attraction of opposites

**Upright**: Union, harmony, relationships, choices, alignment. The Lovers represent sacred union and the choices that shape our path. What values guide your decisions?

**Reversed**: Disharmony, imbalance, misalignment, poor choices. The shadow Lovers are divided within or without.

---

### VII - The Chariot

**Keywords**: Victory, willpower, determination, control
**Archetype**: The Hero
**Hermetic Principle**: Vibration - directed motion

**Upright**: Victory, determination, willpower, control over opposing forces. The Chariot harnesses contradictions into forward motion. What opposing forces can you unite?

**Reversed**: Lack of direction, scattered energy, aggression, defeat. The shadow Chariot is pulled apart by unreconciled opposites.

---

### VIII - Strength

**Keywords**: Courage, patience, inner strength, soft power
**Archetype**: Tamed Instinct
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - gentle strength

**Upright**: Inner strength, courage, patience, soft control. Strength tames the lion not through force but through compassion. Where might gentleness be more powerful than force?

**Reversed**: Self-doubt, weakness, insecurity, raw emotion. The shadow Strength either suppresses instinct entirely or is overwhelmed by it.

---

### IX - The Hermit

**Keywords**: Introspection, solitude, guidance, inner wisdom
**Archetype**: The Seeker
**Hermetic Principle**: Mentalism - inner illumination

**Upright**: Introspection, solitude, inner guidance, wisdom. The Hermit withdraws to find the light within. What truth can only be found in solitude?

**Reversed**: Isolation, loneliness, withdrawal, lost. The shadow Hermit hides from the world or cannot find their inner light.

---

### X - Wheel of Fortune

**Keywords**: Change, cycles, fate, turning points
**Archetype**: Fate
**Hermetic Principle**: Rhythm - cycles and seasons

**Upright**: Change, cycles, destiny, turning points, luck. The Wheel turns - what rises will fall, what falls will rise. Are you riding the wheel consciously?

**Reversed**: Bad luck, resistance to change, breaking cycles. The shadow Wheel finds you fighting inevitable change or trapped in loops.

---

### XI - Justice

**Keywords**: Truth, fairness, law, cause and effect
**Archetype**: Balance
**Hermetic Principle**: Cause and Effect

**Upright**: Justice, truth, fairness, accountability. Justice weighs actions and consequences with clear sight. What truth needs acknowledgment?

**Reversed**: Injustice, dishonesty, imbalance, avoiding accountability. The shadow Justice tips the scales or refuses to see clearly.

---

### XII - The Hanged Man

**Keywords**: Surrender, sacrifice, new perspective, letting go
**Archetype**: Sacrifice
**Hermetic Principle**: Rhythm - pause between beats

**Upright**: Surrender, sacrifice, new perspective, waiting. The Hanged Man sees the world differently because he surrendered control. What might you see if you let go?

**Reversed**: Stalling, resistance, indecision, needless sacrifice. The shadow Hanged Man refuses to surrender or martyrs themselves pointlessly.

---

### XIII - Death

**Keywords**: Endings, transformation, change, transition
**Archetype**: Transformation
**Hermetic Principle**: Vibration - nothing rests

**Upright**: Endings, transformation, change, release. Death clears what no longer serves so new growth can emerge. What needs to die?

**Reversed**: Resistance to change, stagnation, fear of endings. The shadow Death clings to what must pass or rushes endings prematurely.

---

### XIV - Temperance

**Keywords**: Balance, moderation, patience, alchemy
**Archetype**: Integration
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - blending opposites

**Upright**: Balance, moderation, patience, alchemy, integration. Temperance blends opposites into something greater than either. What extremes need balancing?

**Reversed**: Imbalance, excess, lack of long-term vision. The shadow Temperance swings between extremes or mixes incompatible elements.

---

### XV - The Devil

**Keywords**: Shadow, bondage, materialism, limitation
**Archetype**: The Shadow
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - darkness illuminating light

**Upright**: Shadow, bondage, addiction, materialism, limiting beliefs. The Devil shows where we've chained ourselves. What do you believe binds you?

**Reversed**: Release, breaking free, facing shadow, reclaiming power. The shadow Devil either tightens chains or projects evil outward.

---

### XVI - The Tower

**Keywords**: Upheaval, revelation, sudden change, liberation
**Archetype**: Destruction
**Hermetic Principle**: Vibration - sudden shift

**Upright**: Upheaval, revelation, liberation through destruction, truth. The Tower strikes down what was built on false foundations. What needs to fall?

**Reversed**: Resisting necessary destruction, fear of change, personal transformation. The shadow Tower prolongs inevitable collapse.

---

### XVII - The Star

**Keywords**: Hope, faith, renewal, inspiration
**Archetype**: Hope
**Hermetic Principle**: Rhythm - after darkness, dawn

**Upright**: Hope, faith, renewal, inspiration, serenity. The Star pours healing after the Tower's destruction. What renews your hope?

**Reversed**: Despair, disconnection, faithlessness. The shadow Star has lost touch with hope or pours from an empty vessel.

---

### XVIII - The Moon

**Keywords**: Illusion, fear, anxiety, the unconscious
**Archetype**: The Unconscious
**Hermetic Principle**: Polarity - the dark side

**Upright**: Illusion, fear, unconscious, dreams, intuition. The Moon illuminates the hidden path through shadows. What fears distort your vision?

**Reversed**: Release of fear, clarity, truth revealed. The shadow Moon either succumbs to illusion or forces premature clarity.

---

### XIX - The Sun

**Keywords**: Joy, success, vitality, positivity
**Archetype**: Consciousness
**Hermetic Principle**: Mentalism - enlightened mind

**Upright**: Joy, success, vitality, positivity, consciousness. The Sun radiates life-giving clarity. What brings you genuine joy?

**Reversed**: Temporary setbacks, lack of success, inner child wounded. The shadow Sun hides behind clouds or burns without warmth.

---

### XX - Judgement

**Keywords**: Rebirth, calling, absolution, inner calling
**Archetype**: Rebirth
**Hermetic Principle**: Cause and Effect - ultimate accountability

**Upright**: Rebirth, judgement, calling, absolution, awakening. Judgement summons us to rise into our highest self. What is calling you?

**Reversed**: Self-doubt, refusal of calling, harsh self-judgment. The shadow Judgement either ignores the call or condemns without mercy.

---

### XXI - The World

**Keywords**: Completion, integration, accomplishment, wholeness
**Archetype**: Individuation (the integrated Self)
**Hermetic Principle**: All Seven - synthesis

**Upright**: Completion, integration, accomplishment, wholeness. The World celebrates the dance of integration - the Fool transformed. What cycle is completing?

**Reversed**: Incompletion, shortcuts, lack of closure. The shadow World either ends prematurely or cannot release the old cycle.
